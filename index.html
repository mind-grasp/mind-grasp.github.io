MIND-Grasp

Multiview Imagination-guided Next-best-action Discovery for Occlusion-Aware Grasping
Anonymous Submission for CoRL 2026

Overview

MIND-Grasp combines amodal target imagination, occlusion-cloud-guided multiview discovery, and retraction-aware grasp selection to retrieve targets under partial, full, and self-occlusion in cluttered scenes.

Method

Occluded Scene Reconstruction

Amodal Imagination and Observable-Corridor Discovery

MIND-Grasp imagines amodal 3D target geometry under partial and full occlusion, then builds a target-centric occlusion cloud to guide closed-loop multiview discovery through observable corridors.

Retraction-Aware Grasp Selection

Object-Level Retreat and Collision Checking

MIND-Grasp plans an object-level retreat trajectory and selects a grasp whose gripper-target composite body can safely follow it; otherwise, the dominant blocking object is removed and the retreat plan is updated.

NBV Discovery and Retreat

MIND-Grasp performs observable-corridor-guided NBV discovery, plans a safe gripper-target retreat trajectory, and executes the final grasp in cluttered occluded scenes.

Full-Occlusion Discovery

From Hidden Target to Executable Grasp

For fully occluded targets, MIND-Grasp removes the occluder, updates the target amodal 3D model through discovery, and finally grasps the exposed target.

Severe-Occlusion Reconstruction

Single-view reconstruction can produce unreliable geometry and pose under heavy occlusion. MIND-Grasp uses occlusion-cloud-guided NBV planning and multiview updates to recover more complete, pose-consistent target models.

Target-Centric View Planning

NGF often explores globally with longer redundant paths, while VISO-Grasp may resample similar viewpoints. MIND-Grasp focuses on target-centric occlusion clouds, yielding shorter paths with fewer target-critical views.

Limitations and Extensions

MIND-Grasp may be misled when multiple hidden regions are plausible, and its retreat analysis depends on the quality of amodal 3D hypotheses. Future work will maintain uncertainty-aware multi-hypothesis beliefs, improve closed-loop RGB-D correction, and extend parallel-gripper grasp-remove to richer primitives such as pushing, dragging, opening, and dexterous-hand manipulation.
