Abstract

2 remains a central challenge for robotic grasping in cluttered and unstructured environments, where target objects may be partially visible, self-occluded, or completely hidden behind surrounding objects. We present MIND-Grasp, a multi-view imagination-guided next-best-action framework for occlusion-aware grasping. Given a language instruction and eye-in-hand RGB-D observations, the system builds an amodal 3D target hypothesis, reasons about target-centric occlusion structures, and actively selects the next best action to reveal, verify, or grasp the target. Instead of removing all surrounding objects, MIND-Grasp seeks minimal and task-relevant actions by combining multi-view observation, occlusion-cloud-guided view planning, and grasp-retraction feasibility checking. This enables the robot to progressively transition from full occlusion to partial observation and finally to safe target grasping and retrieval.
